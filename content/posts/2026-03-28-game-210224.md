As Crimson Desert approaches an impressive 5 million sales, the CEO of Pearl Abyss, Heo Jin-young, has revealed that the company is exploring the possibility of a Switch 2 port. During a recent shareholder meeting, Heo acknowledged the challenges posed by the current hardware specifications of the Nintendo Switch but expressed a strong interest in developing a version for the upcoming console.

This potential port comes on the heels of Crimson Desert's remarkable sales performance, which saw 2 million copies sold on its first day and 3 million within just four days. While the game has faced some criticism, particularly reflected in a significant drop in Pearl Abyss's share price, the strong sales figures suggest a resilient interest in the title. Heo also mentioned that the team is currently focused on enhancing the base game through free updates rather than paid DLC, indicating a commitment to improving player experience.

With the gaming landscape constantly evolving, the prospect of a Switch 2 version raises intriguing questions about how developers will adapt to new hardware capabilities. Will this lead to a richer gaming experience for players, or will compromises be necessary? Only time will tell as Pearl Abyss continues its development journey.
